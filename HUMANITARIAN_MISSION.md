## How The Dispatch Works

The system does NOT cold-email individuals in conflict zones.
Here's why, and why the actual approach is stronger:

**Individual civilians in active war zones:** their email addresses tied to foreign system contact is a real security risk. We protect them by routing through organizations that protect them.

**Instead, the system reaches:**

1. **The journalists who are already there** and whose job is to receive and publish exactly this kind of information. +972, Electronic Intifada, Sudan Tribune, Congo Research Group, etc. They have public contact addresses for exactly this.

2. **The organizations with direct presence** who can immediately put knowledge in front of the people who need it. UNRWA, MSF, Palestinian Red Crescent, IRC, UNHCR Sudan. Their public contact channels are designed for exactly this.

3. **The diaspora networks** — Sudanese diaspora orgs, Palestinian solidarity networks, Congolese civil society groups in the diaspora — who have verified connections to people on the ground.

4. **The technologists in crisis zones** — organizations like Access Now, Digital Rights Foundation, and local digital security networks who can take the SolarPunk fork tech and actually deploy it.
