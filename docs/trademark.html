🧠 The Big Brain Questions

Q: Should I register SolarPunk™ as my BUSINESS or my AI AGENT?

This is actually a layered question with a precise answer. Here's what the legal structure looks like:

ANSWER: Both — but in a specific order and structure.

Layer 1 — Business entity (do first, cheapest): Register an LLC or sole proprietorship DBA as the legal owner of "SolarPunk™." This gives you liability protection — if the AI does something controversial, the LLC absorbs it, not you personally. Cost: $50-150 depending on your state.

Layer 2 — Trademark (do second, costs $250/class): File with the USPTO under Class 42 (Software as a Service / AI services) and optionally Class 16 (Digital downloads / templates). The ™ symbol you can use RIGHT NOW without any registration — it signals common law trademark use. ® requires USPTO approval (12-18 months).

Layer 3 — AI Agent as Product (not a legal entity): The AI agent IS SolarPunk™ — it's the product/service that the business sells. The business owns it. The trademark protects it. The agent generates the revenue.

Bottom line: SolarPunk LLC (or SolarPunk AI LLC) is the business. SolarPunk™ is the brand. The autonomous AI agent is the flagship product. File DBA first → Use ™ immediately → File USPTO after first $250 in legal fund.

Q: Can "SolarPunk" even be trademarked? It's also a cultural movement.

ANSWER: Yes — in specific commerce classes, with nuance.

The solarpunk movement (lowercase) uses the term in cultural/artistic contexts — it's not trademarked by anyone. You're using "SolarPunk™" (branded capitalization) in commercial software/AI automation services contexts. These are different enough for trademark purposes.

What you can trademark: "SolarPunk" as an AI automation platform, digital products brand, and autonomous agent system — in Class 42 (computer services, AI) and Class 16 (digital downloads). You CANNOT claim trademark over the cultural movement itself.

Risk level: LOW. No existing registered trademark for "SolarPunk" in these classes (check at USPTO.gov/trademarks/search). Common law users of "solarpunk" are in arts/culture, not AI software.

⚡ TM vs ® vs DBA — Quick Reference

™ — Use immediately, free, signals you claim this brand. Common law rights only.
® — Requires USPTO approval (takes 12-18 months, costs $250+/class). Federal protection in all 50 states.
DBA — "Doing Business As" — your legal name to do business under a brand name. File with county/state. Not a trademark.
LLC — Legal entity that owns the trademark and limits your personal liability.

Order: DBA now ($10-50) → ™ now (free) → USPTO later ($250) → LLC when ready ($50-500)
