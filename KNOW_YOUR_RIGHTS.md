## üì± ILLEGAL ROBOCALLS / SPAM TEXTS
**Law:** Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA)
**What it covers:** Unsolicited calls/texts to your cell using auto-dialer. $500‚Äì$1,500 per violation.
**What to do:**
1. Document: screenshot the number, date, time, message content
2. File FTC complaint: reportfraud.ftc.gov (free, takes 5 min)
3. Contact a TCPA plaintiff's firm ‚Äî they work on contingency (free to you):
   - Anderson + Wanca: andersonwanca.com
   - Greenwald Davidson Radbil: gdrlawfirm.com
   - Search: "TCPA class action attorney [your state]"
4. If you got 10+ illegal texts: one firm, one call, potentially thousands in settlement.

## üèõÔ∏è YOUR DATA RIGHTS (USA)
**Law:** California CCPA (if you've ever lived/shopped in CA), various state laws
**What it covers:** Right to know what data companies have, right to delete it, right to opt out of sale.
**What to do:**
1. Send deletion request via email to any company: "Pursuant to [CCPA / your state privacy law], I request deletion of all personal data you hold on me. Name: [your name]. Email: [your email]. Please confirm in writing within 45 days."
2. If they don't comply: file with your state AG office (free)
3. GDPR (if you're in EU or company is EU-based): even stronger. 72-hour breach notification requirement.

## üí≥ ILLEGAL DEBT COLLECTION
**Law:** Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)
**What it covers:** Debt collectors can't call before 8am or after 9pm, can't threaten illegal action, can't harass.
**Each violation:** Up to $1,000 + actual damages + attorney fees (they pay).
**What to do:**
1. Send certified letter: "I dispute this debt and demand you cease all contact."
2. Document every call after that (they're violating the law)
3. Contact FDCPA attorney ‚Äî contingency, free to you:
   - consumerfinance.gov/complaint (file CFPB complaint first)
   - Search: "FDCPA attorney [your state]"

## üè¶ CREDIT REPORT ERRORS
**Law:** Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
**What it covers:** Inaccurate items on your credit report. Damages + attorney fees if not corrected.
**What to do:**
1. Pull free reports: annualcreditreport.com (official, free, weekly)
2. Dispute online with each bureau: Experian, Equifax, TransUnion
3. If not corrected in 30 days: FCRA attorney (contingency, free to you)
4. If you're denied credit, housing, or a job due to an error: sue the bureau.

## üëæ CORPORATE DATA BREACHES
**Law:** State breach notification laws + class action law
**What it covers:** If a company leaked your data (SSN, credit card, medical info) and was negligent.
**What to do:**
1. Check if you're in a breach: haveibeenpwned.com
2. Check active class actions for companies that breached you: classaction.org
3. File a claim (often $50‚Äì$500, no lawyer needed for small claims)
4. For large breaches: contact a class action firm
