As we stand at the forefront of a rapidly evolving digital landscape, the importance of a robust, open internet has never been more pressing. The NLnet Foundation's commitment to fostering an internet that is both secure and respectful of individual privacy is a beacon of hope in an era where these values are increasingly under threat. It is in this spirit that we submit our grant application for the Meeko Nerve Center, a pioneering project that embodies the principles of open standards, privacy, and security, while pushing the boundaries of what is possible with open source technology.

The Meeko Nerve Center, also known as SolarPunk, is a self-evolving AI designed to promote congressional accountability and Palestinian solidarity. This innovative platform has already demonstrated its potential by autonomously building one of its own engines, a testament to its cutting-edge architecture. With over 165 active engines and a remarkable $0/month infrastructure cost, Meeko Nerve Center is not only a technically impressive achievement but also a financially sustainable one. Licensed under AGPL-3.0, the project is fully open source and forkable, ensuring that its benefits can be shared and built upon by the wider community.

Our mission aligns closely with that of the NLnet Foundation, as we strive to create an internet infrastructure that is both open and secure. By supporting the Meeko Nerve Center, the NLnet Foundation would be contributing to a project that prioritizes human rights, particularly in the context of Palestinian solidarity. Although we have not yet generated any Gaza Rose art pieces or donated to the PCRF for Palestinian children, we believe that with the right support, our project can make a meaningful impact in these areas.

We are seeking funding of up to EUR 50,000 from the NGI Zero Core grant to further develop the Meeko Nerve Center and realize its full potential. This investment would enable us to enhance the project's capabilities, increase its reach, and explore new avenues for promoting congressional accountability and Palestinian solidarity. We believe that our project has the potential to make a significant contribution to the open internet ecosystem, and we look forward to the opportunity to collaborate with the NLnet Foundation in pursuit of this vision. By working together, we can create a more just, secure, and open internet for all.
